# FBO 2: Selection Neglect and Strategic Non-Disclosure

## Research Question

Does selection neglect -- where investors drop undisclosed information from their mental model entirely -- create a form of unsophistication that is harder to arbitrage away than the "naive" investors in the existing literature?

## Status

**Stage:** Theory development (first draft of formal model)

## Key Insight

In a trading model (CARA-Normal), the price weight of each investor type depends on perceived precision. Selection neglect investors have artificially high precision (they've dropped a dimension of uncertainty), giving them disproportionate market influence. Moving from a weighted-average framework to a trading model simultaneously *reduces* the impact of naive investors and *amplifies* the impact of selection neglect investors.
